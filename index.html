Frequently Asked Questions

How does sclerotherapy work?

Sclerotherapy is a treatment that is designed to quickly, painlessly, and permanently remove spider veins from legs and has been used for decades. The procedure involves injecting a special solution directly into the vein using a fine needle under ultrasound or fluoroscopic guidance. Once the fluid is injected into the diseased vessel, the vein closes. Over time these vessels will naturally fade away as your body processes them out of the body through liver filtration. The best part about sclerotherapy is that there are very little side effects associated with this minimally invasive treatment.

What are the side effects of sclerotherapy?

Mild side effects can include mild pain , bruising , tenderness, redness and swelling at the injection site.

How long are the treatments?

Treatments require multiple sessions (usually 3-5) spaced every 2 weeks. It usually takes about 40 minutes for each treatment. For most patients, sclerotherapy provides long lasting results - the patient may need touch ups for 6 months or so afterwards . Does sclerotherapy hurt?
