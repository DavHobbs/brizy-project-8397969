Why do legs swell?

There are many causes for leg swelling, but one common cause is poor venous (vein) return from the feet and ankles.  This means the valves in our veins are not working properly which reduces blood flow back up to our heart and lungs.

When this happens fluid leaks out of the veins which accumulate in our feet, ankles, and legs causing us to look like we are swollen.

Even though we might not feel that it is a problem, over time this puts a lot of pressure on the veins and can develop into a more serious condition.

  • Poor veins around ankles
  • Fluid leads out of veins
